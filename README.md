# Manufacturing Workflow

The general workflow used in this project includes the following stages:

1. Selection of anatomical cardiac models representing structural heart diseases.
2. Preparation of digital models for additive manufacturing.
3. Selection of suitable flexible polymeric materials.
4. Fabrication of the physical model using additive manufacturing technologies.
5. Post-processing and preparation of the final educational model.

The complete manufacturing guide is provided in the **manual** directory.

---

# Applications

The models developed within this project are intended for:

- Medical education
- Anatomical training
- Simulation of structural heart conditions
- Communication between healthcare professionals and students
- Training environments for medical device development

---

# Model Source

The anatomical heart models used in this project were obtained from the **3D Heart Project**, which provides segmented cardiac models for educational and research purposes.

3D Heart Project  
https://www.3dheartproject.com

The original models remain the property of their respective creators. This repository only documents the manufacturing methodology used to fabricate physical models for educational use.

---

# Intended Use

The models described in this repository are intended exclusively for:

- Educational purposes
- Training and simulation environments

These models are **not intended for patient-specific surgical planning or clinical decision-making**.

---

# Project Context

This work was developed as part of a graduate research project in the field of **medical device engineering**, focused on the development of accessible cardiac models that can support medical education and simulation.

The objective is to explore fabrication methodologies that enable the creation of realistic cardiac models using flexible polymeric materials and additive manufacturing.
